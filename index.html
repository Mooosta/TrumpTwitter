Art of Speech: Simplicity in communication


Trump is an unusual US president that speaks differently from all his predecessor. He tweets without a second thought, nor cares about potential negative implication or consequence it might cause afterwards. He disregards the fact that his words will be analysed to be used against himself. This is how he uses Twitter to play a part of his persona, even if the meaning below his message is spurious, racist, or incomprehensible (Nerdwriter1, 2015). In this case, Twitter a micro-blogging platform that is just a perfect platform to have his ideas or emotion to be encapsulated within less than 140 characters.


Word Cloud - The scale of each word represents their frequency appearance in Trump's Twitter. (Htoon, 2020)

Trump’s word choice


The word cloud revealed that Trump has a relatively smaller, simpler vocabulary. Other than Pronouns of his favourite, some of his favourite buzz words are "MAGA", "fake", "great", "sad" and "will", each carries a strong emotive value that expresses his emotion easily. He loves rhythmic talk, continuous repetition of his favourite buzz words, in conjunction with ending his tweets on strong punchy keyword. All these small details aid him to express his stance on implicated topic easily, implicating the listener agreed on his point, framing negative response as over-reaction, in doing so embeds his ideas deep into the reader's impression.

Language Structure


The scale below displays some of the US political figures' language readability level. The number is an indication of how complex the speech can be understood and engaged with, it is the result of a mathematical calculation with the variable of word choices, sentence and language structure. The number represents the minimum education grade level to understand a particular speech. the higher the grading the more sophisticated their language is.

Most presidential candidates using words and grammar typically between grades 6-8, though Donald Trump tends to lag behind the others, which is 4.1, the lowest out of all 2016 candidates (Spice, 2021). According to an analysis by Carnegie Mellon University, Trump favours to use fewer characters per word in his announcement speech, fewer syllables per word, and his sentences were shorter than all other candidates. For election hopefuls, simpler language resonates.

Sentiment Tweeter: Emotive Speaker


As a former reality show host and New York City real estate developer, sure Trump has never run for political office but knows how to use sentiment to manipulate reader emotions, As discussed previously in his word usage, he often emphasise his sentiment stance at the end of his of Tweets, to express favour or disfavour towards readers by using positive or negative language, It's is persuasion speech techniques used to control reader's emotional reaction. ("Detecting Stance in Tweets And Analyzing its Interaction with Sentiment", 2016) Working his whole life as a business salesman, this natural knack for selling the feeling gains popularity with his speech. Viewers can understand his message needless to spend extra time and efforts to decipher the meanings behind it.

As the cluster shown above, there is often either more strong positive or negative sentiment than neutral statement presented in Trump's Tweet. For a someone who has no previous political background, and constantly at the top of controversial discussion, many commentators dismiss Trump's chances many people have a lot to speak about his personality and views. It is undeniable that he is successful and capable in achieve constant world attention and popularity with his speech. Silently shifting social norms and stereotypical ideology of how politician talk, disposing of difficult words or euphemism and using relatable language to create connections and bring together citizens.

Difference in speaking connotation


For example, compare to 44th United of States President - Barack Obama tweets in a cleaner and eloquent manner whilst Trump talks more informal and emotional, his tweet rather gives a colloquial feel, and resembles how normal people uses social media. As discussed in the previous section, Trump favours speaking in a short sentence, fewer characters and syllables per word as possible. Trump may not be a smart nor informed speaker, but is witty and has good comedic sense, relatable language that resonates better with the public.

Been underestimated, Trump was successful in utilising Twitter as an effective medium to communicate with the crowd throughout his presidency. This allows reflection: Twitter is a constantly changing system especially the nature of political discourse on the platform subtle shifts in the distribution of users' preference in content like more spam and less in-depth conversation. Being straightforward and lowering the language comprehension barrier is the new key to have ideas perceived by the crowd.

Conclusion: Change of pace in communication


Seeing the underlying trend, Trump took the opportunity to grasp the psychology that American citizens are tired of hearing politicians' constantly being sophisticated-sounding, yet accomplish little or nothing. Trump been elected was a change of pace in the political world, break of stereotypical politician impression. His whole 'Twitter career' can be summarised up with White House Spokesman's description "people critiquing his use of it (controversial tweets) now critiqued it during the election and it turned out pretty well for him".
